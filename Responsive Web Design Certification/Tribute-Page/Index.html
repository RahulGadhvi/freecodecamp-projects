Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, was a leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. He is also known as the "Father of the Nation" in India. Gandhi's philosophy of nonviolent resistance played a crucial role in India's struggle for freedom. His commitment to peace, justice, and equality inspired millions worldwide. Through acts of civil disobedience, such as the famous Salt March in 1930, he led the Indian people in challenging oppressive British policies. Gandhi's teachings continue to resonate globally, advocating for truth, tolerance, and the power of nonviolence in achieving social and political change.
